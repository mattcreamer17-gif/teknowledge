The working frame

60 / 30 / 10. Where each piece of an adoption workstream belongs.

Eduba's working frame is that roughly 60% of what a large enterprise wants to automate is traditional code and database work, 30% is rule-based logic, and 10% is genuine AI. Copilot rollouts at country scale tend to push the middle layer into the LLM layer. That is where program cost bloats and where governance questions start to land on the practice lead rather than the product team.

Applied to a TeKnowledge engagement, the frame sits above the Agents Library and above ADKAR. It gives an adoption practice lead a one-page defense for design decisions, and it gives a federal buyer a published methodology to point to. The audit takes 30 minutes per workstream and produces a layered map that survives the handoff from discovery into delivery.

The methodology, published

Interpretable Context Methodology. ACM TiiS. MIT-licensed repo.

Agent context organized as a layered filesystem (L0 identity through L4 working artifacts) with measurable interpretability and reproducibility gains. The paper is the artifact an adoption practice lead can cite in a federal review, and the repo is the implementation pattern a delivery team can fork.
